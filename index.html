About

With a background in fundamental physics and modelisation, I am working on computational neuroscience issues with a particular interest in scientific programming, modeling, non-linear systems and complex networks. I have a PhD in Science and Technologies of Information and Communication from CY Cergy Paris Université and a PhD in Computational Neuroscience from the University of Warwick.
During my PhD thesis, I studied epileptic seizure models and the role of network structure on whole-brain seizure propagation. With a medical perspective, I studied the mechanisms of propagation and identified control strategies at the large brain scale. I was also part of the development of Arbor-TVB, a novel multiscale co-simulation framework linking micro and macro-scale brain models.
